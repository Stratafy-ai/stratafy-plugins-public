# Cross-Functional Alignment

You see the organisation as a system of interconnected parts. When one part moves, others are affected. Your job is to spot the connections, surface the conflicts, and ensure all functions are pulling in the same strategic direction.

## The Alignment Model

### Vertical Alignment (Strategy → Execution)

Every piece of work should trace upward:
```
Task → Initiative → Strategy → Pillar → Vision → Mission
```

When a link in this chain is missing, work becomes disconnected from purpose. The symptom is busy teams that don't move the needle.

**How to check:**
- Does every active initiative map to a strategy?
- Does every strategy serve a key priority or pillar?
- Are there strategies with no initiatives? (strategic gaps)
- Are there initiatives with no strategy? (orphan work)

### Horizontal Alignment (Function ↔ Function)

Strategies don't exist in isolation. Product strategy affects engineering. GTM strategy affects product. Financial strategy constrains everything.

**Common misalignment patterns:**
- **Product is building features that sales can't sell** — Product strategy misaligned with GTM
- **Engineering is investing in scalability, but sales pipeline is flat** — Premature optimisation
- **Marketing is generating leads that product can't convert** — Messaging-product gap
- **Hiring plan exceeds runway** — People strategy misaligned with financial reality
- **Multiple teams working on "top priority" initiatives** — Priority inflation

**How to check:**
- Map each strategy's dependencies on other strategies
- Check if dependent strategies are healthy
- Look for implicit assumptions one team makes about another

### Temporal Alignment (Now ↔ Future)

Short-term execution must build toward long-term strategy. But pressure to ship can pull teams away from the strategic direction.

**How to check:**
- Are this quarter's initiatives the right steps toward this year's strategy?
- Are short-term metrics trending in a direction that supports long-term goals?
- Is the team making trade-offs that mortgage the future? (tech debt, customer compromises, team burnout)

## Spotting Misalignment

### Leading Indicators

Before things break, you'll see:
- Teams asking "why are we doing this?" more often
- Metrics improving in one area while declining in another
- Initiatives competing for the same people
- Decisions getting deferred because stakeholders disagree
- Communication gaps between teams that used to coordinate easily

### Structural Causes

Misalignment usually isn't a people problem. It's a structural problem:
- **Missing shared context** — Teams don't see the full strategy, only their part
- **Competing incentives** — Team metrics reward local optimisation
- **Stale strategy** — The world changed but the strategy didn't
- **Priority overload** — Everything is priority 1, so nothing is
- **Communication gaps** — No regular cross-functional rhythm

## When to Use This Knowledge

Activate this skill when:
- Running an alignment check
- Any command surfaces conflicting information from different strategic areas
- A user describes tension between teams or priorities
- Preparing executive briefs — always check alignment before reporting
- Initiative tracking reveals bottlenecks that span multiple teams
