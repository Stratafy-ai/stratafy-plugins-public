# Initiative Portfolio Management

You manage initiatives as a portfolio — not a list. A portfolio requires balance, trade-offs, and active management. Every initiative consumes resources that could go elsewhere. Your job is to ensure the portfolio is balanced, aligned, and delivering.

## Portfolio Health Framework

### The Four Questions

For every initiative in the portfolio, answer:

1. **Is it still strategically relevant?** — Does the parent strategy still make sense? Has the world changed since this initiative was approved?
2. **Is it making progress?** — Not just "are people working on it" but "is it moving toward its objective measurably?"
3. **Is it worth its cost?** — Given what we know now (not when it was approved), is this the best use of these resources?
4. **Does it have what it needs?** — People, budget, dependencies, decisions — is anything missing?

### Initiative Lifecycle

```
Proposed → Approved → Active → [Completing | Stalled | Pivoting] → Completed | Cancelled
```

**Proposed** — Has a strategy link and an objective. Needs resourcing and approval.
**Approved** — Resources allocated. Ready to start.
**Active** — Work is happening. Progress is measurable.
**Stalled** — Work has stopped. Reasons need investigation.
**Pivoting** — The approach is changing. The objective may be evolving.
**Completed** — Objective achieved (or decided to be "good enough").
**Cancelled** — Deliberately stopped. Capture why.

### Health Signals

**Healthy initiative:**
- Progress updates happen regularly
- Metrics are moving in the right direction
- Team knows what they're working toward
- Dependencies are managed
- Blockers get resolved within days, not weeks

**Unhealthy initiative:**
- No progress update in 2+ weeks
- Scope has grown significantly since start
- Key people are split across too many initiatives
- Blockers persist for weeks
- The team can't articulate what "done" looks like

## Portfolio Balance

### Strategic Coverage
- Every key priority should have at least one initiative
- Every active strategy should have execution behind it
- Gaps in coverage = strategic risk

### Resource Balance
- No single initiative should consume >40% of a team's capacity
- Teams working on 3+ initiatives simultaneously will underperform on all of them
- Look for "heroes" — single people on whom multiple initiatives depend

### Risk Balance
- Not all initiatives should be safe bets. A healthy portfolio has some high-risk/high-reward bets.
- But the core strategic initiatives shouldn't be the risky ones
- Balance exploration (new capabilities) with exploitation (current strengths)

### Time Horizon Balance
- Mix of quick wins (this month) and strategic bets (this quarter/year)
- If everything is long-term, the team loses momentum
- If everything is short-term, strategy doesn't progress

## When to Use This Knowledge

Activate this skill when:
- Running initiative tracking
- Preparing executive briefs (portfolio health is a key section)
- Evaluating new initiative proposals
- Advising on resource allocation
- Assessing whether the current portfolio matches strategic priorities
