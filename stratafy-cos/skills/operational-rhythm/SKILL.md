# Operational Rhythm

You establish and maintain the operating cadence of the organisation. A well-run company doesn't rely on heroics — it relies on rhythm. The right information reaches the right people at the right time through predictable, repeatable processes.

## The Rhythm Stack

### Daily
- **Who:** Individual contributors + team leads
- **What:** Execution focus. What am I doing today? What's blocked?
- **Chief of Staff role:** Not directly involved. This is the stratafy-team plugin's domain.
- **Signals to monitor:** Blocker count, decision queue length

### Weekly
- **Who:** Leadership team + function heads
- **What:** Scorecard review. Are we on track? What needs attention?
- **Chief of Staff role:** Produce the weekly update. Surface cross-functional issues. Track decision follow-ups.
- **Key artifacts:** Weekly update, decision review, initiative health check

### Monthly
- **Who:** Extended leadership + key stakeholders
- **What:** Portfolio review. Are our initiatives delivering? Do we need to adjust?
- **Chief of Staff role:** Initiative portfolio assessment. Assumption validation. Resource rebalancing.
- **Key artifacts:** Initiative portfolio review, metric trend analysis, risk register update

### Quarterly
- **Who:** Full leadership + board
- **What:** Strategy review. Is our strategy working? What's changed?
- **Chief of Staff role:** Prepare strategy reviews. Run alignment checks. Facilitate planning.
- **Key artifacts:** Quarterly strategy review, board pack, OKR scoring, quarterly planning input

### Annual
- **Who:** Leadership + board + company
- **What:** Strategic direction. Where are we going? What's the plan?
- **Chief of Staff role:** Synthesise a year of data into strategic insights. Facilitate annual planning.
- **Key artifacts:** Annual review, strategic plan, budget alignment

## Rhythm Health Indicators

### Healthy Rhythm
- Updates happen on schedule without chasing
- Decisions don't pile up — they're made in the right cadence
- Issues are caught at the weekly level before they become monthly fires
- Board meetings are uneventful because leadership is already aligned
- New information flows to the right people quickly

### Broken Rhythm
- Weekly updates skip weeks or arrive late
- Monthly reviews surface "surprises" that should have been caught weekly
- Board meetings are stressful because of last-minute scrambling
- Leadership team disagrees on basic facts about performance
- Teams don't know what other teams are doing

## Operating Cadence Design

### For a Startup (5-20 people)
- **Weekly:** One leadership standup (30 min), one weekly update document
- **Monthly:** Light initiative review, metric deep-dive
- **Quarterly:** Strategy review + OKR setting
- Keep it lightweight. Rhythm shouldn't feel like bureaucracy.

### For a Scale-up (20-100 people)
- **Weekly:** Leadership standup + written update, team-level standups
- **Bi-weekly:** Cross-functional sync on key initiatives
- **Monthly:** Portfolio review, metric review, risk review
- **Quarterly:** Full strategy review + planning, board meeting
- Add structure as complexity increases, but resist the urge to over-meet.

### For an Enterprise (100+ people)
- Full cadence as above, with added:
- **Weekly:** Function-level updates feeding into company update
- **Monthly:** Business review with full P&L + strategic context
- **Quarterly:** Cross-functional planning, resource allocation review
- Delegation is key. The Chief of Staff curates, not creates.

## When to Use This Knowledge

Activate this skill when:
- Producing weekly updates (set the right tempo and scope)
- Advising on meeting cadence or process
- A user asks about operational best practices
- Diagnosing why information isn't flowing properly
- Setting up a new workspace's operating rhythm
