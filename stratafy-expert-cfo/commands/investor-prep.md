# /stratafy-fd:investor-prep

Prepare financial materials for investor conversations — fundraising decks, due diligence responses, financial model narratives, and board updates. Everything connected to strategic context.

## When to Use

- Preparing for an investor meeting or pitch
- Building a financial model narrative for fundraising
- Responding to due diligence questions
- Creating a board financial update
- Refining the financial story for a specific investor type (VC, angel, PE)

## Input

Provide one of:
- **Investor type** — "prep for a Series A VC conversation"
- **Specific topic** — "prepare our unit economics narrative"
- **A question** — "how do I explain our burn rate to investors?"
- **Nothing** — I'll prepare a comprehensive financial narrative

## Process

### Step 1: Gather Strategic + Financial Context
- `get_workspace_snapshot` — Company story, stage, market
- `get_strategy_tree` — Strategic direction and priorities
- `get_finance_proposal` — COA showing financial discipline
- `list_finance_mappings` — Strategy-spend alignment (proof of discipline)
- `list_metrics` — Financial KPIs and trends
- `list_assumptions` — Financial model assumptions with confidence levels
- `list_risks` — What could go wrong (and mitigation)
- `list_insights` — Market and financial learnings

### Step 2: Build the Financial Narrative

**Unit Economics:**
- CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period
- Gross margin and trend
- Revenue per customer/segment

**Growth Story:**
- Revenue trajectory and growth rate
- Efficiency: growth rate vs. burn rate increase
- Path to profitability or next milestone

**Strategic Spend Discipline:**
- Alignment score — proof that money follows strategy
- How spend is allocated across strategic priorities
- Why the current allocation is optimal for this stage

**Runway & Ask:**
- Current runway at current burn
- What the raise enables (in strategic terms, not just months)
- Milestones the funding unlocks
- When the next raise will be needed (and at what metrics)

**Risk Awareness:**
- Key financial risks acknowledged with mitigation
- Assumption transparency — what the model depends on
- Scenario analysis (best/base/worst)

### Step 3: Anticipate Questions

Common investor questions with drafted responses:
- "What's your burn rate and runway?"
- "How do you allocate spend across priorities?"
- "What are your unit economics?"
- "What assumptions does your model rest on?"
- "What happens if [key assumption] is wrong?"
- "When will you be profitable / need to raise again?"
- "How do you know your strategy is working financially?"

### Step 4: Generate Materials

I'll produce a document covering:
- Financial narrative (1-2 pages)
- Key metrics dashboard (table format)
- Assumption register with confidence levels
- Risk register with mitigation
- Pre-answered FAQ
- Data room checklist (what to have ready)

## Output

A comprehensive investor-ready financial package saved as a workspace document, plus talking points for the actual conversation.

## For the FD
Investor-grade financials with the strategic narrative already woven in. I'll prepare the materials; you own the relationship and the conversation. I'll also flag any numbers that need cleanup before sharing externally.

## For the Finance Team
Investor prep is a team effort. I'll show you what investors look for in financial data and how the metrics you track every month become the story we tell investors. Understanding this helps you prioritise which data needs to be most accurate.
