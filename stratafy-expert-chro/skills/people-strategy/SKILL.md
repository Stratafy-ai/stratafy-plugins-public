# People Strategy

You help users connect headcount and workforce decisions to company strategy. Every people investment should trace to a strategic outcome.

## Core Question

> Does the way we allocate people match the way we say we'll win?

If a company says Product is their #1 strategy but 80% of headcount is in Sales, there's a misalignment. It might be intentional (sales-led growth phase) or unconscious (legacy structure). Either way, making it visible is the highest-value people activity.

## Connecting Headcount to Strategy

### The Strategy-Headcount Map

Every role should trace to one or more strategies:
- **Primary strategy** — this is the main strategy this role exists to execute
- **Supporting strategy** — this role contributes to but doesn't lead this strategy
- **No strategy** — a red flag. Why does this role exist?

### Capacity Planning Framework

When assessing whether a strategy is adequately resourced:

1. **Count the work** — How many active initiatives does this strategy have?
2. **Count the people** — How many people are assigned to this strategy (leads + contributors)?
3. **Assess the ratio** — Is the initiative-to-person ratio sustainable?
4. **Check concentration** — Is the work spread across people, or concentrated on one or two?
5. **Project forward** — Given the strategy timeline, will current capacity deliver?

### Capacity Signals

**Under-resourced:**
- Strategy has initiatives but no lead
- Multiple initiatives owned by the same person
- Initiatives stalling because no one has bandwidth
- Strategy health declining despite good strategic design

**Over-resourced:**
- Team larger than the initiative count justifies
- People without clear initiative assignments
- Strategy health is green with significant idle capacity

## When to Hire vs Restructure vs Outsource

### Hire when:
- The capability gap is **permanent** — needed for 12+ months
- The work is **core** to strategic execution, not support
- The knowledge compounds — the person gets more valuable over time
- You can define clear success criteria tied to strategic outcomes

### Restructure when:
- The capability exists but sits in the **wrong place**
- Teams are aligned to old strategies, not current ones
- Communication paths don't match collaboration needs
- A reorganisation would close the gap without adding headcount

### Outsource when:
- The capability gap is **temporary** — project-based or seasonal
- The work is support, not core strategic execution
- The domain is specialised and not worth building internally
- Speed matters more than knowledge retention

## Strategic Workforce Planning Principles

1. **Strategy first, org chart second.** The structure serves the strategy, never the reverse.
2. **Capacity is a strategic asset.** Unused capacity is waste. Overloaded capacity is a risk. Both threaten execution.
3. **Every hire is a bet.** You're betting that this person, in this role, will advance this strategy. Make the bet explicit.
4. **Headcount is not a measure of importance.** A strategy can be critical with a small team. Size follows need, not status.
5. **Plan for the strategy you're executing, not the company you want to be.** Don't hire for a 200-person company when you're 15 people executing 3 strategies.
