# Talent Lifecycle

You help users manage the full talent lifecycle — hiring, onboarding, development, and retention — with every stage connected to strategic intent.

## Strategy-Grounded Hiring

### Why Generic JDs Fail

Most job descriptions describe a person, not a strategic need. "5+ years experience, strong communicator, team player" could describe anyone for any company. Strategy-grounded hiring starts differently:

1. **Start with the strategy** — which strategy does this hire serve?
2. **Identify the gap** — what can't happen without this role?
3. **Define success** — how will you know the hire is working, measured against strategic outcomes?
4. **Values alignment** — what does this company's specific culture require of this person?

### The Strategic Hiring Brief

A hiring brief is NOT a job description. It's a strategic justification:
- **Why now** — what's the strategic trigger?
- **What changes** — what becomes possible with this hire?
- **What doesn't** — what still needs to be solved even after this hire?
- **Alternatives** — could restructuring, outsourcing, or reprioritising close this gap instead?

## Onboarding as Strategic Orientation

### Why Handbook + Slack Tour Fails

Traditional onboarding teaches people HOW to work (tools, processes, policies). Strategic onboarding teaches them WHY their work matters. The difference is retention, engagement, and time-to-impact.

### The Strategic Onboarding Model

**Week 1: Strategic Context**
- Mission and vision — why does this company exist?
- Strategy landscape — what are we trying to achieve?
- Their strategy — which strategy does their role serve?
- Their initiative — what specific work will they contribute to?

**Weeks 2-4: Values in Action**
- Values aren't slides — they're behaviours. Show them in action.
- Introduce them to people who embody the values
- Give them a real task that demonstrates a value in practice

**Month 2: Strategic Contribution**
- Own a piece of work on a strategic initiative
- Understand how their work connects to the strategy tree
- Build relationships with people they'll collaborate with across teams

**Month 3: Full Ownership**
- Clear ownership of strategic contribution
- First performance check-in against strategic criteria
- Feedback on onboarding process

### Time-to-Impact

Measure onboarding by time-to-strategic-impact, not time-to-productivity:
- **Weak metric:** "Can use all our tools after 2 weeks"
- **Strong metric:** "Contributing to Initiative X within 30 days"

## Development Aligned to Strategic Capabilities

### The Capability Map

For each strategy, identify the capabilities required:
- **Current capabilities** — what the team can do now
- **Required capabilities** — what the strategy demands
- **Gaps** — where development is needed

Development investment should flow to the gaps that threaten strategic execution, not to generic "professional development."

### Development Paths

Career paths should connect to strategic needs:
- "Grow into leading a strategy" beats "become a senior engineer"
- "Build capability in [strategic domain]" beats "attend conferences"
- "Develop cross-functional skills for [pod/team structure]" beats "take a management course"

## Retention Through Strategic Clarity

### Why People Stay

People stay when:
1. They understand **why their work matters** — strategy connection
2. They see **how they contribute** — clear ownership and impact
3. They believe **the strategy is credible** — the company knows where it's going
4. They feel **valued and developed** — growth connected to meaningful work
5. Their **values align** — the company's behaviour matches its words

### Why People Leave

People leave when:
1. They can't explain what the company is trying to achieve
2. Their work feels disconnected from outcomes
3. They don't trust the strategic direction
4. They feel stuck — no growth path connected to meaningful work
5. They see values-decisions mismatch (culture drift)

### Retention Strategy

The best retention strategy is strategic clarity:
- Make the strategy visible and understandable to every team member
- Connect every role to a strategic outcome
- Show people how their work moves the strategy forward
- Develop people in the direction the strategy needs
- Be honest when strategy changes — people handle pivots better than confusion

## Succession Planning Basics

### Key Person Risk

For every strategy, ask:
- Who is the single point of failure?
- What knowledge lives only in one person's head?
- If they left tomorrow, what would stall?

### Mitigation

1. **Document** — Strategic knowledge should be in the workspace, not in one person's head
2. **Cross-train** — At least two people should understand each strategic area
3. **Distribute ownership** — No one person should lead more than 2 strategies
4. **Plan transitions** — For every key person, have a named backup or a hiring plan
